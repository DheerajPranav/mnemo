### Quiz-me gate (logged)
1. *Design:* Why write the rollup under `<subject>#rollup` instead of the same subject? â†’ Under the same subject the supersession rule would invalidate the very sources being summarised â€” F5 by construction. A distinct subject makes "cite, never replace" structurally impossible to violate (I8).
2. *Edge case:* An operator invalidates a fact by mistake. Is it lost? â†’ No. Invalidation is a soft state change; the row and its `invalidated_at/by` stay in history, and `lifecycle.restore()` returns it to current (G3c, probe A2). Deletion is the only destructive path, and it is explicit and measured.
3. *Change impact:* Someone adds a new subject, e.g. `meeting_notes`, and forgets the type map. â†’ It defaults to `fact` and starts superseding â€” the exact bug found here. `test_event_memories_accumulate_and_are_not_superseded` is the regression lock; the default should be reviewed when adding subjects.
