The one slide

The scan confirmed the Deliverable 1 design more than it overturned it, with one sharp addition. Adopt a bi-temporal validity model and move conflict resolution off the ranker (external evidence: Zep). Adopt an evaluation scaffold so the non-negotiable baseline comparison is buildable (LongMemEval). Adopt a testable PII admission gate (Presidio). Prototype one admission mechanism (Mem0). Defer or reject the rest, including one SOTA result that breaks a core invariant. And a real 2026 paper argues the whole system is unnecessary — it is answered, and it improved the plan.

3. Highest-value opportunity: bi-temporal validity (adopt)

Problem addressed
F4 — a superseded fact served as current. inference first_principles.md §6 makes the entire case for multi-signal ranking (C5, the component that "does the most work") hinge on this failure being real.
Mechanism
Each typed fact carries valid-from / valid-to (world time) and recorded-at / invalidated-at (system time). A contradicting fact invalidates the prior one (sets its valid-to) instead of deleting it. Retrieval filters to currently-valid facts; history is kept for audit and re-derivation.
Evidence, and how far it transfers
verified Zep reports up to +18.5% on LongMemEval and DMR 94.8% vs 93.4% against MemGPT, crediting temporal handling. inference The setting (assistant chat + business data) is closer to GTM than Generative Agents' simulation but still not multi-tenant or regulated, so the mechanism transfers while the magnitude does not.
The real design move
Not just a schema field: relocate conflict resolution from the ranker to the write path. The ranker then only ever orders facts that are all currently valid, which is cleaner than asking a relevance+recency score to encode validity — something failure_analysis.md D-1 argued it structurally cannot do.
Why adopt the model but not the engine
The full Graphiti temporal knowledge graph would violate the v1 non-goal of hand-specified, inspectable structure and add heavy operational surface. The bi-temporal fields and invalidation semantics capture the benefit at a fraction of the cost.
Validation (bounded, in Deliverable 3)
Three-arm comparison on the fixed set: naive similarity vs similarity+recency vs validity-filter. Metric: how often a superseded preference is served over the current one.
What would reverse it
to measure If similarity+recency matches the validity-filter on the fixed set, P4 does not force this in practice and it is downgraded to defer. This is falsifier 1 from Deliverable 1, now wired into the Day-3 protocol.

4. Two supporting adoptions

4.1 LongMemEval as the evaluation scaffold (adopt taxonomy + subset, extend)

C10 needs a fixed adversarial set and baseline comparison is a non-negotiable gate. LongMemEval's five abilities include knowledge updates (= F4) and abstention (= cold-start / no-relevant). inference Adopt its taxonomy and case designs as the backbone; it has no cross-tenant (F10) or PII (F11) abilities, so those GTM-critical cases are authored on top. Guard: never imply its coverage extends to isolation. (ADR-002.)

4.2 Presidio PII gate at admission (adopt deterministic-first)

F11 is unrecoverable and needs a tested control. inference Deterministic recognizers first (cheap, no LLM on the PII path, no injection surface), NER optional and measured. Recognizer recall is imperfect, so deletion (C7) stays as the backstop — the gate is defence-in-depth, not a sole guarantee. (ADR-003.)

5. The challenge that made the plan better

Steelman — "none of this is necessary"

verified Back to Basics (arXiv:2604.11628, 2026) argues the bottleneck is a Signal Sparsity Effect, not memory architecture, and that a minimalist retrieval+generation pipeline beats stronger memory baselines on token/latency cost. inference It does not refute this design: its benchmarks are recall tasks; it says nothing about F10 cross-tenant leakage, F11 incidental PII, or F9 tested deletion — the unrecoverable failures that motivate the system. A better retriever cannot satisfy an isolation invariant at any accuracy.

But it changed two things. (1) The Day-3 baseline must be a strong minimalist retriever (Turn-Isolation Retrieval), not a straw man — a fairer, harder baseline to beat. (2) It sharpened falsifier 1: if the minimalist retriever already handles knowledge-updates on the fixed set, the temporal model is not earning its cost and gets cut. I now hold the temporal model as adopt-pending-measurement, not near-certain.
